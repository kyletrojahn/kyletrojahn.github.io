Welcome! I am an incoming Ph.D. Student in the Department of Government at The University of Texas at Austin. With subfields in American Politics & Public Policy, I specialize in the policy agenda-setting of sub-national political institutions. I am particularly interested in the influence of partisan polarization on the policy agendas of local school boards and city councils.

I'm proud to be a first-generation college student and product of public education at the K-12, undergraduate, and graduate level. My academic journey began at Truman State University in Kirksville, MO. Here, I received a B.S. in Political Science & International Relations (with honors and Phi Beta Kappa). At Truman, I conducted a variety of research projects on sub-national, national, and international education policy. Following completion of my undergrad, I moved back to my hometown and joined Washington University in St. Louis' Department of Education as the Faculty Support Coordinator.

You can reach me at trojahn@utexas.edu.
